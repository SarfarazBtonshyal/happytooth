Root Canal Treatment
◆  Specialist Endodontics  ·  MDS Endodontist on site

Most people come in dreading a root canal — and leave wondering why they waited so long. When the pulp inside a tooth becomes infected or inflamed due to deep decay, a crack, or repeated dental procedures, a root canal is the only way to save that tooth without resorting to extraction. Left untreated, the infection spreads, causes severe pain, and eventually destroys the bone around the root.
At HappyTooth Smile Studio, root canal treatment is performed by Dr. Thushara Sudhakaran, our in-house MDS Endodontist — a specialist who has dedicated her postgraduate training specifically to this field. The procedure uses a rotary endodontic system with nickel-titanium files that are more flexible and precise than manual instruments. Where cases warrant it, magnification is used to improve accuracy during canal preparation and cleaning.
Most straightforward cases can be completed in a single appointment. Complex teeth — molars with additional canals, curved roots, calcified canals — may require a second visit. After treatment, the tooth is sealed and restored with a crown to protect it from fracture under biting forces.
Who needs it:  Patients with toothache that is severe or worsening, lingering sensitivity to heat or cold, pain on biting, swelling near a tooth, or a tooth that has gone dark after trauma.
What to expect:  Local anaesthesia, no pain during the procedure, mild soreness for 1–2 days post-treatment. Most patients return to normal the next day.
Why here:  Having a dedicated MDS Endodontist on site — not a referral elsewhere — means complex root canals are handled with specialist-level expertise from the first appointment.

Smile Designing
◆  Cosmetic  ·  Restorative  ·  Customised Treatment Planning

Your smile is usually the first thing people notice, and it shapes the impression you make — consciously or not. Smile designing is not a single procedure but a carefully planned combination of treatments that work together to achieve the result you want. It might involve veneers, crowns, whitening, gum correction, orthodontic adjustments, or a mix of these — depending entirely on what your teeth and face need.
We begin with a thorough evaluation of your teeth, their alignment, the shape and position of your gums, and how your smile interacts with your lips and facial proportions. We then create a digital smile preview so you can see the anticipated outcome before any clinical work begins. This step is non-negotiable — you approve the design, and only then do we proceed.
Smile designing is not about making everyone look the same. A well-designed smile looks natural, fits your face, and enhances rather than overwhelms your features. The result should look like the best version of your own teeth — not someone else's.
Common concerns addressed:  Stained or discoloured teeth, chipped or uneven edges, visible gaps, asymmetry, a gummy smile, worn or short teeth, old or mismatched restorations.
Treatment timeline:  Varies by case. Simple smile makeovers (whitening + veneers on front teeth) can be completed in 2–3 visits over 2–3 weeks. More comprehensive cases involving multiple teeth and gum correction may take 4–8 weeks.
First step:  A consultation and smile assessment — no commitment required at that stage.

Aligners & Braces
◆  Orthodontics  ·  Children & Adults

Crooked, crowded, or gapped teeth are among the most common dental concerns we see — and they matter beyond aesthetics. Misaligned teeth are harder to clean, wear unevenly over time, and can contribute to jaw joint problems and bite-related headaches. Orthodontic treatment corrects these issues by moving teeth into their proper positions over a planned period of time.
We offer three main orthodontic options. Traditional metal braces remain the most reliable and cost-effective choice for significant corrections — they work efficiently across all age groups and allow precise control for complex tooth movements. Ceramic braces function the same way but use tooth-coloured brackets, making them far less conspicuous while delivering equivalent results. Clear removable aligners are the preferred choice for adults who want to straighten their teeth without visible appliances — they are worn throughout the day, removed only for eating and cleaning, and changed every 1–2 weeks as treatment progresses.
For children, early orthodontic assessment (from around age 7–8) allows us to identify developing bite problems and address them at a stage when correction is simpler and faster. For adults, age is not a barrier — teeth can be moved at any age as long as the gums and bone are healthy.
Treatment duration:  Mild corrections: 6–9 months. Moderate cases: 12–18 months. Complex full-arch alignment: 18–24+ months.
Aligners vs. braces:  Aligners are suitable for mild to moderate misalignment. Braces are preferred for more complex corrections, rotations, and significant bite issues. Your assessment will clarify which option applies.
Retention:  After active treatment, retainers are essential to hold results. This phase is permanent but requires minimal effort — usually just nighttime wear after the first year.

Teeth Whitening
◆  Cosmetic  ·  In-Clinic Professional Treatment

Tooth colour naturally changes over time. Tea, coffee, red wine, certain spices, and decades of everyday use leave behind staining that regular brushing simply cannot remove. The result is teeth that look dull or yellow — not because of poor hygiene, but because of surface and subsurface discolouration that requires a clinical-grade whitening agent to address.
Professional in-clinic whitening delivers noticeably brighter teeth in a single appointment — a result that over-the-counter whitening strips and trays cannot match, both in terms of degree of whitening and the speed at which it happens. Before treatment, we assess your enamel condition and gum health to confirm that whitening is appropriate for your case. Sensitivity, existing restorations, and the type of staining all factor into how we approach your treatment.
The whitening agent is applied carefully to the tooth surfaces with gum protection in place, and activated over a controlled period. Results are calibrated to your starting shade — the aim is teeth that look naturally bright, not artificially white. For patients who want to maintain results over time, a take-home maintenance kit can be provided after the in-clinic treatment.
Best candidates:  Patients with extrinsic staining (surface) from food, drink, or tobacco. Also effective for natural age-related yellowing. Less suitable for tetracycline staining or discolouration caused by internal tooth issues — these cases are better addressed with veneers.
Longevity:  Results typically last 12–24 months with normal dietary habits. Avoiding heavy tea, coffee, and tobacco significantly extends the effect.
Note:  Crowns, veneers, and composite fillings do not whiten. If you have visible front-tooth restorations, discuss this during your consultation so we can plan accordingly.

Paediatric Dentistry
◆  Children's Dental Care  ·  Ages 1 and above

A child's first few visits to the dentist have a lasting effect on how they feel about dental care for the rest of their life. A frightening early experience can lead to avoidance that causes real harm over years. That is why we approach children's dentistry differently — with unhurried appointments, a calm environment, and a manner that helps children feel safe rather than anxious.
We see children from the eruption of their first tooth. Early appointments are as much about familiarity as they are about clinical examination — getting the child comfortable with the chair, the lights, and the dentist before any treatment is ever needed. As they grow, we monitor tooth development, watch for crowding or bite problems, and treat cavities in milk teeth before they affect the permanent dentition underneath.
Preventive treatment is a major part of what we do for children. Pit-and-fissure sealants are a particularly effective intervention — a thin protective coating applied to the grooves of the back teeth, where decay almost always begins. Fluoride application strengthens enamel during the years when it matters most. For children with habits like thumb sucking, mouth breathing, or tongue thrusting, we provide habit-breaking appliances and counselling before these habits affect jaw development.
Services for children include:  First dental check-ups, cavity treatment in milk teeth, pit-and-fissure sealants, fluoride application, space maintainers, habit correction appliances, and early orthodontic assessment.
First visit age:  Ideally by age 1, or within 6 months of the first tooth erupting. At the latest, before age 3.
For parents:  We encourage parents to stay in the room during the appointment. A relaxed parent helps the child stay calm.

Dental Implants
◆  Restorative  ·  Permanent Tooth Replacement

A missing tooth is more than a gap in your smile. Within months of losing a tooth, the bone underneath begins to resorb — it shrinks because there is no longer a root stimulating it. Neighbouring teeth tilt into the space. The opposing tooth over-erupts. Biting becomes uneven, and over years this creates a cascade of problems that become harder and more expensive to fix.
A dental implant addresses all of this. A small titanium post is placed into the jawbone where the root of the missing tooth used to sit. Over 3–6 months, the bone fuses around it in a process called osseointegration. Once this is complete, a ceramic crown is attached to the implant — one that is shaped and shaded to match your surrounding teeth, and functions exactly like a natural tooth. You brush it, floss around it, and eat with it without any restriction.
Every implant case at our clinic begins with a proper assessment. Digital X-rays confirm bone availability and anatomy. Cases involving limited bone height or proximity to the sinus or nerve require cone beam computed tomography imaging for safe and precise planning. We do not proceed without a thorough understanding of your bone structure.
Candidacy:  Most adults in good general health are candidates. Adequate bone volume is the primary clinical requirement. Uncontrolled diabetes, active gum disease, and smoking significantly affect implant survival and must be addressed before placement.
Timeline:  3–4 months healing after placement (longer if bone grafting is needed), followed by crown fitting. Total: typically 4–6 months from placement to final restoration.
Longevity:  Implants, when properly placed and maintained with regular cleaning and check-ups, routinely last 15–25+ years. Many last a lifetime.

Crowns & Bridges
◆  Restorative  ·  Fixed Prosthetics

A dental crown is a full-coverage restoration that fits over a damaged or weakened tooth, restoring its original shape, size, strength, and appearance. It is the appropriate treatment when a tooth has too little natural structure remaining for a filling to be reliable — after a root canal, a large fracture, or severe wear. A crown protects the remaining tooth structure and allows the tooth to function normally under biting forces.
A bridge fills the gap left by a missing tooth without implants. It consists of a false tooth (called a pontic) anchored on both sides by crowns placed over the adjacent teeth. The result is a fixed, non-removable restoration that looks and functions like natural teeth. A well-constructed bridge, properly maintained, typically lasts 10–15 years or more.
We work primarily with zirconia and tooth-coloured ceramic materials. Zirconia is exceptionally strong — suitable for back teeth that bear high biting forces — and is also highly aesthetic when used for front teeth. Full-ceramic crowns on front teeth offer the most natural-looking result, with light transmission properties that closely mimic natural enamel. The material choice depends on the location of the tooth, the bite forces involved, and your aesthetic expectations.
Crown procedure:  Two appointments in most cases. First visit: tooth preparation and impression. Second visit (10–14 days later): crown fitting and cementation. A temporary crown is worn in between.
Bridge vs. implant:  A bridge is faster and lower in cost but involves preparing the adjacent teeth. An implant preserves neighbouring teeth completely and is a more independent solution. We discuss both options with you before proceeding.
Aftercare:  Crowns and bridges require normal brushing plus flossing under the bridge pontic using a floss threader. Regular check-ups confirm fit and gum health around the restoration.

Dental Veneers
◆  Cosmetic  ·  Porcelain & Composite

Veneers are ultra-thin ceramic or composite shells, custom-fabricated and permanently bonded to the front surfaces of the teeth. They are one of the most versatile cosmetic procedures in dentistry — capable of addressing chipped or worn edges, uneven tooth shapes, persistent staining that does not respond to whitening, small gaps, and minor misalignment that would otherwise require orthodontic treatment.
Porcelain veneers are crafted in a dental laboratory from pressed or layered ceramic. They are colour-stable, highly resistant to staining, and possess a translucency that closely mimics natural enamel. When bonded properly, they are remarkably durable. Composite veneers are applied directly in the clinic using tooth-coloured resin — they can be completed in a single appointment and are more economical, but are less stain-resistant and may need replacement or refinishing after 5–7 years.
The design phase is as important as the clinical execution. We assess your tooth proportions, gum line, midline, and overall facial symmetry before designing the final shape and shade. A wax-up or digital preview is prepared for your review before any tooth preparation begins. Preparation for porcelain veneers involves removing a thin layer of enamel from the front surface — this is permanent and cannot be reversed, which is why the planning stage is so important.
Best results for:  Front teeth with staining, chips, minor misalignment, worn edges, or size discrepancies. Veneers are typically placed on the upper front 6–10 teeth.
Not suitable for:  Patients with severe misalignment (orthodontics is a better first step), teeth grinding without management, or teeth with insufficient enamel remaining.
Longevity:  Porcelain veneers: 10–20 years with good care. Composite veneers: 5–8 years. Both require avoiding excessive biting force on the bonded surfaces.

Gum Care & Periodontics
◆  Periodontics  ·  Gum Disease Treatment

Gum disease is the leading cause of tooth loss in adults, and it is almost entirely silent in its early stages. Most patients do not notice it until it is already causing significant damage. The warning signs — bleeding during brushing, gums that look swollen or darker than usual, a persistent unpleasant taste, or teeth that appear longer because the gums have receded — are often dismissed as minor issues. They are not.
In its early stage (gingivitis), gum disease is fully reversible with professional cleaning and improved oral hygiene. Once it progresses to periodontitis, the supporting bone around the teeth is lost — and bone does not regenerate on its own. The aim at this stage shifts from reversal to arrest and management.
Our gum treatment services range from thorough scaling and root planing (deep cleaning below the gum line, usually under local anaesthesia) to periodontal flap surgery for advanced cases where surgical access is needed to properly debride deep pockets. After active treatment, a structured maintenance programme — typically a professional clean every 3–4 months — is essential to prevent recurrence.
The link between gum disease and systemic health is well established. Untreated periodontitis is associated with poorer blood sugar control in diabetic patients, increased cardiovascular risk, and complications in pregnancy. This is not incidental — it reinforces why gum treatment is a medical priority, not just a cosmetic one.
Signs of gum disease:  Bleeding on brushing, red or puffy gums, bad breath that persists after cleaning, gum recession, teeth feeling loose, food packing between teeth.
Treatment approach:  Diagnosed by clinical examination and dental X-rays. Mild-moderate disease: deep cleaning in 1–2 sessions. Advanced disease: surgical intervention may be required.
Maintenance:  Lifelong periodontal maintenance (3–6 monthly) is the only reliable way to keep gum disease from recurring once it has been treated.

Scaling & Polishing
◆  Preventive  ·  Routine Oral Health Maintenance

A professional clean every six months is the most cost-effective thing you can do for your oral health — and yet it is routinely postponed or skipped entirely by most people. The reasoning is understandable: if nothing hurts, why bother? The answer is that by the time something hurts, the problem is usually much larger than it needed to be.
Scaling removes calculus — the hardened mineralised deposits that form when plaque is not fully removed by brushing. Calculus cannot be removed at home, regardless of technique or toothbrush type. Once it forms, it creates a rough surface that attracts more bacteria, triggers gum inflammation, and accelerates bone loss around the teeth. Polishing follows scaling and removes surface staining from tea, coffee, and tobacco while smoothing the enamel surface to make plaque adhesion harder.
Beyond the mechanical cleaning, the real value of this appointment is what we observe during it. Early cavities, beginning gum pockets, cracked enamel, soft spots in the teeth — these are all visible on a clean tooth under good light. Identifying them at an early stage means simpler, cheaper treatment. What would have been a small filling at six months can become a crown at two years if left undetected.
Frequency:  Every 6 months for most adults. Patients with a history of gum disease, heavy calculus buildup, or diabetes should attend every 3–4 months. Children benefit from annual professional cleans from the time their permanent teeth erupt.
Duration:  Typically 45–60 minutes, including clinical examination and dental charting.
After the appointment:  Some patients experience mild sensitivity for 24–48 hours, particularly where the gums were inflamed before scaling. This resolves quickly and is a sign the gum tissue is responding positively.

Extractions & Wisdom Teeth
◆  Oral Surgery  ·  Simple & Surgical Extractions

When a tooth is too damaged to save — by decay, fracture, failed previous treatment, or severe infection — extraction is the right decision. Attempting to preserve a tooth beyond its clinical viability leads to ongoing pain, spreading infection, and bone loss that ultimately makes future tooth replacement more difficult. A well-timed extraction, followed by an appropriate replacement plan, is the better outcome.
Wisdom teeth are a separate and very common problem. Most adults do not have enough space in their jaws for all four third molars to erupt fully and correctly. The result is an impacted tooth — one that is partially or fully trapped beneath the gum line, often growing at an angle that pushes against the adjacent molar. Impacted wisdom teeth cause episodic pain, infection in the surrounding gum (pericoronitis), and can gradually damage the roots of the neighbouring tooth if left in place.
Before any extraction, we take the appropriate radiograph — a periapical or orthopantomogram depending on complexity — to assess root anatomy, proximity to the inferior alveolar nerve in lower wisdom teeth, and surrounding bone density. Straightforward extractions are completed under local anaesthesia in a single appointment. Surgical extractions, where bone removal or tooth sectioning is required, take longer but are equally manageable under appropriate anaesthesia and, where needed, sedation.
Recovery:  Simple extractions: discomfort for 2–3 days, normal eating within a week. Surgical wisdom tooth removal: swelling and discomfort for 4–7 days, full recovery within 2 weeks in most cases.
Post-extraction care:  Bite on gauze for 30 minutes, avoid hot food and tobacco for 24 hours, no rinsing on the first day. Detailed written instructions are provided after every extraction.
Replacement planning:  Extracted teeth (other than wisdom teeth) should be replaced to prevent adjacent tooth shifting. We discuss implant, bridge, or denture options at the time of extraction.

Dentures & Full Mouth Rehabilitation
◆  Prosthodontics  ·  Complete & Partial Tooth Replacement

Significant tooth loss — whether from decay, gum disease, trauma, or a combination of these — affects far more than appearance. Chewing becomes restricted, which affects nutrition and digestion. Speech changes. The jaw muscles and joints adapt to the new bite in ways that cause pain over time. Self-confidence diminishes in ways that people often don't openly discuss but universally feel.
Complete dentures replace all teeth in an arch. They rest on the gum ridges and, when fabricated with precision, provide a natural appearance and acceptable function for most daily activities. The fit improves with time as the patient adapts, and periodic adjustment and relining keep them comfortable as the jaw ridge changes shape over years.
Partial dentures replace some but not all teeth in an arch, using the remaining natural teeth for support and retention via clasps or precision attachments. They are removable and maintain the position of remaining natural teeth by preventing them from drifting into the gaps.
For patients who want the stability and permanence of fixed teeth, implant-supported dentures offer the most significant upgrade in quality of life. Two to four implants placed in the jaw support a fixed or removable prosthesis that does not move, does not require adhesive, and allows normal biting force. Full mouth rehabilitation combines implants, crowns, bridges, and when necessary orthodontic treatment to restore both the function and aesthetics of a severely compromised dentition.
Timeline:  Conventional dentures: 4–6 appointments over 4–6 weeks. Implant-supported prosthetics: 4–9 months from placement to final delivery, depending on healing.
Full mouth rehabilitation:  Typically 3–9 months depending on case complexity, number of missing teeth, and whether bone grafting is required.
Maintenance:  Removable dentures require nightly removal and cleaning. Implant-supported restorations are maintained like natural teeth — brushing, flossing, and regular professional check-ups.

Digital X-Rays & Diagnostics
◆  Diagnostics  ·  Radiation-Reduced Digital Imaging

Every treatment decision we make is only as good as the diagnosis behind it. Accurate, high-quality imaging is not optional — it is the foundation of safe dental care. We use digital radiography as our standard system for intraoral and panoramic X-rays.
Digital X-rays produce sharper, higher-resolution images than conventional film, are available on screen within seconds, and deliver significantly lower radiation exposure — typically 80–90% less than conventional film X-rays. Images can be enhanced, zoomed, and compared across appointments to track changes over time. This is particularly valuable for monitoring early bone loss, interproximal cavities between teeth, and the progression or stability of lesions.
For more complex diagnostic situations — implant planning, impacted wisdom teeth, suspected cysts or pathology, jaw joint assessment, or cases involving unclear root anatomy — cone beam computed tomography scanning provides a three-dimensional volumetric image of the jaws and teeth. This level of detail allows us to assess bone height and density, identify critical anatomical structures like the inferior alveolar nerve and maxillary sinus, and plan treatment with a precision that two-dimensional X-rays simply cannot provide.
Intraoral digital X-rays:  Used for individual teeth — cavities, root assessment, bone level check, post-root canal verification.
Orthopantomogram (OPG):  Full-mouth panoramic X-ray showing all teeth, both jaws, temporomandibular joints, and surrounding structures. Used for overview assessment, wisdom teeth evaluation, implant planning.
Cone beam computed tomography:  3D cross-sectional imaging for surgical planning, complex implant cases, and pathology assessment. Available on referral where indicated.

Emergency Dental Care
◆  Emergency  ·  Same-Day Appointments for Acute Cases

Dental emergencies do not wait for convenient appointment slots. A toothache that builds through the night, a tooth knocked out during a fall, a crown that comes off before an important event, a dental abscess that is causing facial swelling — these are situations where waiting is the wrong choice. Delayed treatment makes most dental emergencies significantly worse.
We keep appointment capacity for emergency cases every day. If you are in acute pain or have sustained dental trauma, call us and explain what has happened. We will assess the urgency, advise you on any immediate steps to take, and get you in as quickly as possible — the same day in most cases.
Common emergency situations we handle include severe toothache and dental abscesses, fractured teeth from trauma or biting on hard objects, teeth that have been knocked loose or completely avulsed (knocked out), lost or broken crowns, bridges, or fillings that are causing pain or discomfort, and soft tissue injuries to the gums or lips. For an avulsed tooth, immediate action gives the best chance of reimplantation — keep the tooth moist (in milk or saline, never tap water) and come in immediately.
Dental abscess:  A spreading infection that causes swelling, fever, or difficulty swallowing is a medical emergency — go directly to a hospital emergency department if swelling is rapidly progressing or affecting breathing.
Pain management:  We will advise on appropriate over-the-counter pain relief while you are on your way. Do not place aspirin directly on the gum — this causes a chemical burn and does not relieve pain.
After-hours:  For after-hours emergencies, our contact details and emergency guidance are available on our website. For life-threatening situations involving airway compromise or severe facial trauma, call emergency services first.
