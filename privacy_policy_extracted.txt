PRIVACY POLICY
HappyTooth Smile Studio
Murukkumpuzha, Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala  —  happytoothdental.in
Effective: June 2026  •  Version 1.0


This policy explains what personal and health information HappyTooth Smile Studio collects, why we collect it, how we protect it, and what your rights are. We handle all patient data with the care and discretion that medical information requires, and in compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA) and the IT (Reasonable Security Practices) Rules, 2011.
1.  Who We Are
HappyTooth Smile Studio is a specialist dental clinic at Murukkumpuzha, Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala. The clinic is led by Dr. Thushara Sudhakaran (BDS, MDS, Endodontist). We are the data controller for all patient and visitor information collected through the clinic and our website, happytoothdental.in.
2.  What We Collect
Personal details — name, date of birth, address, phone number, and email.
Health and clinical data — medical history, dental examination findings, diagnosis, treatment records, X-rays, and intraoral photographs.
Financial records — treatment fees paid, billing history, and mode of payment.
Appointment history — visit dates, recall records, and communication logs.
Website enquiry data — name, phone, or email submitted through contact or booking forms on our website.

Health data is classified as Sensitive Personal Data or Information (SPDI) under Indian law. We apply the highest standard of protection to this category.
3.  Why We Collect It
To provide dental examination, diagnosis, and treatment.
To maintain clinical records as required by law and professional regulations.
To process appointments, send reminders, and manage billing.
To respond to your queries and complaints.
To comply with legal obligations — tax records, clinical documentation, and regulatory requirements.

We do not use your information for commercial advertising, third-party marketing, or any purpose unrelated to your dental care and our clinic's lawful operations.
4.  How We Store and Protect Your Data
Patient records — clinical notes, X-rays, treatment history, and contact details — are managed through Dentobees, a dental practice management software. Dentobees stores data on secured servers and provides role-based access controls that limit visibility to authorised clinical and administrative staff only.
Paper records, consent forms, and medical questionnaires are stored in locked cabinets within the clinic premises. Access is restricted to clinic staff. All staff are required to maintain patient confidentiality as a condition of their engagement.
5.  Who We Share Your Information With
Treating clinicians within our team who are directly involved in your care.
Dental laboratories — for fabrication of crowns, veneers, dentures, or aligners. Only the clinical prescription and necessary technical details are shared; no personal contact information beyond a patient reference is provided.
Referring specialists or hospitals — when we refer you for specialist care, we share the relevant clinical records with your knowledge.
Dentobees (practice management platform) — as a data processor handling records on our behalf, under contractual confidentiality obligations.
Legal or regulatory authorities — only when required by a valid court order, law enforcement request, or regulatory obligation.

We do not sell, rent, or share your information with any marketing company, insurance aggregator, or commercial third party.

7.  Your Rights
Access — you can request a copy of your personal and clinical records at any time.
Correction — you can ask us to correct inaccurate personal details. Clinical records are amended with the original entry preserved.
Erasure — you can request deletion of non-clinical personal data that is no longer necessary. Clinical records within the mandatory retention period cannot be deleted.
Withdraw consent — you may withdraw consent for processing at any time. This does not affect treatment already provided.
Grievance — if we do not resolve your complaint satisfactorily, you may approach the Data Protection Board of India once constituted under the DPDPA, 2023.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us in writing using the details in Section 9. We respond within 7 working days.
8.  Minor Patients
For patients below 18, clinical consent is obtained from a parent or legal guardian. Medical history forms are completed by the accompanying parent. Records of minor patients are accessible to the parent or guardian with parental responsibility. Once the patient turns 18, control of their records transfers to them. Records are retained until the patient turns 25, or 5 years from last treatment, whichever is later.
